About College


Gafargaon Government College is a government college located in Gafargaon town of Mymensingh district. The college is located on the banks of the old Brahmaputra river in the city's college road. The college was established in 1950, converted into a degree college in 1965 and nationalized in 1985. This college has played an important role in the expansion of higher education in Gafargaon. This college was established 68 years ago to expand education in Gafargaon. The college was upgraded to a degree college in 1965. The college was officialized on 1st July 1985.

The number of departments in the college is 14. At present the number of teachers is 48. At present 1 professor, 4 associate professors, 5 assistant professors and 18 lecturers are teaching. There is a student hostel for the accommodation of female students. The college library is enriched with more than 6,000 books.

Gafargaon Government College has more than hundred species of trees, vines and shrubs. This college has preserved Kumphi plants of Bangladesh. There are also Arborai, Arjuna, Ata, Amlaki, Ashphal, Kadam, Kanakchura, Kalke, Kamini, Keya, Krishnachura, Karamcha, Kath Badam, Jackfruit, Gagan Shirish, Golapjam, Chapalish, Chalta, Chatim, Jarul, Jayati, Jam, Jamrul, Pomegranate, Fig, Deua, Tamal, Palm, Tamarind, Desi Amra, Nagalingam, Nageshwar, Nishinda, Coconut, Palash, Guava, Bot, Bakul, Bilimbi, Muchkunda, Menda, Mankanta, Mahua, Fig, Lukluki, Lychee, Lohakath, Sharifa , safeda, betel nut etc.
